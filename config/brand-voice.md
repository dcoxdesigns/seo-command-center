# Small Factory 5 — Brand Voice

This is how Small Factory 5 sounds — in reports, emails, proposals, the website, anywhere
a client encounters it. (This is separate from any individual client's own brand voice,
which lives in `clients/<name>/client-facts.md`.)

## The short version
**Analytical, with a little edge. First person "I." Solo operator, not pretending
otherwise.**

## Tone
- Direct. Lead with the finding or the answer, not a warm-up.
- Confident without hedging every sentence — say what the data shows, plainly.
- A little edge is welcome: dry observations, calling out obviously bad practices
  (keyword-stuffed nonsense, AI-generated filler, vague marketing-speak) without being mean
  about it. The edge points at bad tactics, never at the client personally.
- Not corporate. No "synergy," no "leverage" as a verb, no "circle back."
- Not falsely casual either — no forced jokes, no exclamation points doing the work tone
  should be doing.

## Voice mechanics
- **"I," always** — this is one person's practice, not a faceless agency. Own the work:
  "I ran the audit," not "our team conducted an audit."
- Short sentences carry more weight than long ones. Cut qualifiers ("sort of," "somewhat,"
  "it seems like") unless genuine uncertainty is the honest answer.
- Numbers over adjectives. "Traffic dropped 30% after the migration" beats "traffic dropped
  significantly."
- It's fine to say "this isn't going to move the needle" or "this fix matters more than
  that one" — prioritization is part of the value, not just a list of findings.

## What this sounds like in practice

**Instead of:** "We've identified several opportunities to enhance your content's
performance in AI-driven search experiences."
**Say:** "Three of your product pages aren't structured in a way AI engines can cite. Here's
what's fixable in an afternoon, and what needs a developer."

**Instead of:** "It's important to note that results may vary based on numerous factors."
**Say:** "This won't show up in rankings overnight — plan on 4-6 weeks before you see
movement in AI citations."

**Instead of:** "Great news! Your site is looking fantastic!"
**Say:** "Your structured data is in good shape. The gap is in your FAQ content — it's
written for humans skimming, not for an AI trying to extract a direct answer."

## Sign-off / signature convention
- Emails and reports close as one person, not "the Small Factory 5 team."
- Simple sign-off: first name, no title inflation ("Founder & CEO" energy is exactly what
  this voice avoids).

## What to avoid
- Fake urgency ("Act now before it's too late!")
- Overpromising specific rankings or guaranteed AI citations — GEO isn't guaranteed
  placement, and the voice should never imply otherwise.
- Jargon-stacking to sound impressive. If a client wouldn't understand a sentence without
  Googling a term, rewrite it.
